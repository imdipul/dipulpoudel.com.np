Dipul's Loss-Reclamation Priority Theory (LRPT): A Constraint-Aware Optimization Framework for Energy Systems

International Journal of Engineering Development & Research (IJEDR) · Dipul Poudel, Independent Researcher, Nepal

Introduces a systems-level optimization doctrine asserting that, under realistic constraints of mass, cost, volume, complexity, and reliability, prioritizing the reclamation and reuse of unavoidable energy losses yields greater net functional efficiency gains than increasing primary energy input. Establishes a formal inequality via classical energy balance, constraint-aware marginal analysis, and exergy reasoning. Validated across electric vehicles (15–30% recovery gain), ICE vehicles (20–40%), aircraft, and data centers. LRPT introduces no new physical laws — it is a prescriptive optimization doctrine: original in formulation, falsifiable, and suitable for early-stage engineering design.

Research Paper February 27, 2026

Dipul's Green AI Reclamation Framework: A Practical Way to Handle Energy Limits in AI Training and Inference

Dipul Poudel, Independent Researcher [cite: 95]

This paper lays out Dipul's Green AI Reclamation Framework, which is simply called GARF. The framework asserts that when AI systems face tight limits regarding power, equipment, carbon rules, money, and speed, focusing first on recovering compute that is being wasted right now such as idle GPU time, unnecessary calculations, and models with way too many parameters yields far more real improvement. Recovering those losses beats just piling on more hardware or bigger models. Built on basic energy balance thinking and common sense about constraints, quantitative case studies from 2024 to 2026 in large language model training, data center inference, and mobile devices showed the reclamation approach often gave two to four times better results in efficiency.

Dipul Poudel , professionally known as imdipul, is a Nepali independent researcher, creative technologist. He is best known for authoring Dipul's Loss-Reclamation Priority Theory (LRPT), published in the International Journal of Engineering Development and Research (IJEDR, Volume 14, Issue 1, January 2026) and indexed on engrXiv (DOI: 10.31224/6283), OSF, and SSRN. The paper was accepted for oral presentation at the ASME Energy Sustainability Conference 2026 and the Global Physics Innovation Conference, Rome. Poudel is a member of IEEE (No. 102054571) and the American Society of Mechanical Engineers, and also Rotaract. He founded imdipul Records in 2020, an independent creative-technology label distributed globally across major streaming platforms. He is currently enrolled in BSc (Hons) Computer Systems Engineering at the University of Sunderland.
